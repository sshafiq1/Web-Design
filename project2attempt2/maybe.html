In Which A Pirate Does Not Find Gold

Struck suddenly by the cold wet weight of the wave, Boldbeard was washed overboard. As air left his lungs, all Boldbeard could think of was who would stop his crew from taking all of his booty when he was gone. A few days later, his body was picked up by passing fishermen. He would have been happy to know that, at least to the sheriff, his body was worth its weight in gold.
